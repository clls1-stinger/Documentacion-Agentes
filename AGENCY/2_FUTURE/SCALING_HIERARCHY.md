# 🚀 SCALING HIERARCHY & CAPACITY ANALYSIS

> **Directive**: Determine the limits of the current infrastructure and define the order of investment for the business phase.

## 1. 📊 CURRENT CAPACITY (THE BOTTLE-NECKS)

### 📡 Network (700 Mbps Simétrico / 4TB Cap)
*   **Simultaneous Users (Bandwidth)**: At 3.13 Mbps/stream, your line can support **~220 simultaneous users** theoretically.
*   **The REAL Limit (Data Cap)**: 4TB / month.
    *   3.13 Mbps = 1.4 GB / hr.
    *   4,000 GB / 1.4 GB = **~2,850 hours of playback per month**.
    *   **User Limit**: If each user watches 2 hours/day (60 hrs/mo), you can support **~47 users** before hitting the cap.

### 💿 Disk (PUSKILL M.2 1TB)
*   **IOPS/Speed**: 1.8 GB/s is massive. The disk is NOT the bottleneck for streaming. It could handle thousands of concurrent YTS streams.

## 2. 🏗️ PRIORITIZED UPGRADE HIERARCHY

This is the order in which we must reinvest the first earnings to scale the business safely:

### 🥇 Priority 1: Unlimited Connectivity
*   **Action**: Upgrade to 1Gbps Simétrico (Symmetrical) **without data cap**.
*   **Reason**: The 4TB limit is the "Visible Hand" that will kill the service as soon as it gains traction. This is the first professional step.

### 🥈 Priority 2: Primary Cache Expansion (New M.2)
*   **Action**: Buy a 2TB or 4TB M.2 NVMe (Samsung/Crucial for high endurance).
*   **Reason**: We need a dedicated, larger cache to store localized (Latino) and popular content without constant deletion/re-downloading.

### 🥉 Priority 3: Bulk Storage (SATA HDD / NAS)
*   **Action**: High-capacity HDD (12TB+ Enterprise).
*   **Reason**: To move from "Streaming/Cache" to a "Permanent Library" where users can find everything without waiting for downloads.

### 🏅 Priority 4: Redundancy (Second Node)
*   **Action**: Mirror the setup on a second machine.
*   **Reason**: High availability (Business continuity).
